VMS Platform Comparison

Nx Witness vs Genetec Security Center — 197 Camera Site
Prepared by: Meld Strategies
Author: Bruce Duyshart, Founder & Principal
Date: 30 April 2026
Document type: Research Report

Executive Summary

This report compares two leading Video Management Systems (VMS) — Nx Witness by Network Optix and Genetec Security Center (Omnicast) — for a 197-camera deployment in the Australian market. Nx Witness is positioned as a lightweight, cost-efficient platform with a once-off perpetual licensing model and strong open-API extensibility. Genetec is positioned as an enterprise-grade unified security platform with deep federation, mapping, access-control, and analytics integration, available as either perpetual on-premise or SaaS.

For a 197-camera site, indicative total Year-1 spend is approximately AUD $80,000–$110,000 for Nx Witness Professional and approximately AUD $200,000–$280,000 for Genetec Security Center Pro/Enterprise. Genetec carries a 2–3× cost premium that is typically only justified where federation across multiple sites, integrated access control (Synergis), or advanced operational workflows are required. Nx Witness is the recommended choice for single-site or simple multi-site deployments where total cost of ownership and ease of administration are priorities.

1. Background & Scope

Meld Strategies has been asked to provide an indicative comparison between two CCTV VMS platforms for a single-site deployment of 197 IP cameras. The intent is to inform early-stage platform selection, not to replace a vendor-supplied quotation. All pricing is indicative AUD ex-GST and based on publicly available reseller pricing and industry-standard SMA/SaaS models as at April 2026.

Assumed deployment characteristics:

  • 197 IP cameras (mix of 2MP and 4MP, ONVIF Profile S/T compliant)
  • Single site, single management domain
  • 30-day continuous recording at 8 fps with H.265 compression
  • Estimated raw storage requirement: approximately 90–120 TB usable
  • 2–4 simultaneous operator workstations
  • No integrated access control (CCTV-only) at this stage

2. Platform Overview

2.1 Nx Witness (Network Optix)

Nx Witness is a Russian-origin (now US-headquartered) VMS released in 2014, built on the Nx Meta platform. It is widely distributed in Australia through resellers including Ness Corporation, Security Wholesalers, ISCS, and SecurityAustralia. The platform is offered in Professional, Enterprise, and Global editions, plus an Nx Cloud SaaS layer for remote access and federation.

2.2 Genetec Security Center / Omnicast

Genetec is a Canadian unified security platform vendor founded in 1997. Security Center is the unified platform; Omnicast is the video module. The platform is available in Standard, Professional, and Enterprise editions for on-premise deployment, plus Security Center SaaS for cloud-managed deployments. Streamvault appliances (SV-1000E, SV-2000E, SV-4000E) are pre-configured turnkey servers. In Australia, Genetec is delivered through certified integrators including JD Security, Thinkbox Solutions, and Virgo Security.

4. Pricing Models — Structure

4.1 Nx Witness Pricing Model

  • Per-camera perpetual licence — one-off purchase, no expiry. Tiers: Professional (~AUD $195/camera ex-GST, retail), Enterprise (~AUD $295/camera, advanced analytics & AI features), Global (~AUD $395/camera, multi-site federation).
  • Free version updates for the life of the licence — no mandatory annual renewal.
  • Optional Nx Cloud subscription for remote access, federation, and cloud relay (typically free for ≤5 users, paid tiers for enterprise).
  • No server licence — servers are unlimited; only cameras are counted.
  • Hardware — runs on commodity Windows/Linux servers or any compatible appliance; no proprietary hardware required.
  • Reseller margin — typical 15–25% discount off list to integrators, sometimes passed on.

Indicative pricing referenced from SecurityAustralia and ISCS public retail listings (April 2026); actual project pricing depends on volume, reseller and bundle.

4.2 Genetec Pricing Model

  • Per-camera connection licence — perpetual, requires an active SMA (Software Maintenance Agreement) for updates. Indicative ex-GST list:
    • Standard: ~AUD $200–$250/camera
    • Professional: ~AUD $400–$500/camera
    • Enterprise: ~AUD $600–$750/camera
  • Base Directory licence — one-off ~AUD $3,500–$6,000 plus optional failover.
  • Annual SMA — typically 18–22% of licence value per year for updates and support.
  • Streamvault appliances — pre-loaded servers from ~AUD $9,000 (SV-1000E, ~50 cameras) to ~AUD $25,000+ (SV-4000E, 300+ cameras).
  • Security Center SaaS — subscription per camera per month (~AUD $35–$55/camera/month indicative); Streamvault Edge appliance still required onsite for storage. No perpetual licence; ongoing OPEX.
  • Certified integrator delivery — only Genetec-certified integrators may deploy; commissioning and design services typically 15–25% of software value.

Genetec does not publish AUD list pricing; figures above are derived from publicly disclosed US/EU pricing benchmarks (B&H, Insight) plus typical AU integrator margin and currency conversion. Actual quotes from JD Security, Thinkbox Solutions, or other certified integrators will vary.

7. Discussion & Recommendation

7.1 Where Nx Witness wins

  • Significantly lower TCO — ~40–55% cheaper than equivalent Genetec tier on a 5-year basis.
  • No mandatory annual maintenance cost — predictable budget; updates remain free.
  • Lightweight architecture, runs on commodity hardware (Windows or Linux).
  • Faster to deploy and configure; less specialist training required.
  • Strong fit for commercial buildings, industrial, retail, and education sectors with single-site or simple multi-site needs.

7.2 Where Genetec wins

  • Native federation across many independent systems — the gold standard for city-scale, transport, port, and government deployments.
  • Tightly integrated unified platform: Omnicast (video) + Synergis (access) + AutoVu (LPR) + Mission Control (operations) under one client.
  • Superior cybersecurity posture (FIPS 140-2, hardened Streamvault, regular ASD/IRAP-relevant validation).
  • Mature analytics ecosystem (KiwiVision, Cloudrunner, AutoVu) with deep Australian integrator support.
  • Better suited to environments with strict compliance, complex operational workflows, or future need for unified access control.

7.3 Recommendation for a 197-camera site

  1. Default position: Select Nx Witness Professional — best value, sufficient feature depth for almost all single-site CCTV use cases at this scale, lowest TCO over 5 years.
  2. Step up to Nx Witness Enterprise if native object/forensic search and AI-driven analytics are core requirements.
  3. Choose Genetec Omnicast Professional only if any of the following apply:
    • The site is part of a federated multi-site or city-wide system (e.g. precinct, transport, government campus).
    • Integrated access control via Synergis is required now or within 3 years.
    • Cybersecurity certification (PSPF, IRAP) is a hard requirement.
    • The client has an existing Genetec estate and standardisation is mandated.
  4. Avoid Genetec SC SaaS at the 197-camera scale unless the client has an explicit OPEX preference — on-premise perpetual is ~50% cheaper over 5 years.

8. Risks, Caveats & Next Steps

  • All pricing in this report is indicative and based on public retailer listings, published US/EU price benchmarks, and standard AU integrator margins. It must not be used as a tendered or contracted price.
  • Genetec does not publish AUD pricing — firm quotes must be obtained from a certified Australian integrator (e.g. JD Security, Thinkbox Solutions, Virgo Security).
  • Storage sizing assumes 30-day retention at 4 Mbps blended bitrate; longer retention or higher resolutions will materially shift hardware cost for both options.
  • Cybersecurity, NCC compliance and any AS 4806 (Closed Circuit Television) requirements should be re-confirmed against the specific project brief before final selection.
  • Recommended next step: issue a short-form RFI to two Nx Witness resellers and two Genetec certified integrators with the 197-camera scope, retention requirement, and any access-control/analytics requirements to obtain firm AUD quotes.
