Smart Building Technology Assessment
Leighton Properties — 200 George Street, Sydney
Prepared for: Bruce Duyshart
Date: 6 April 2026
Prepared by Meld Strategies

Executive Summary

This Smart Building Technology Assessment evaluates the current and planned building technology systems at 200 George Street, Sydney, against contemporary best-practice benchmarks for commercial office assets. The property demonstrates strong foundational infrastructure but has identifiable gaps in system integration, occupant experience platforms, and sustainability data reporting. Meld Strategies recommends a prioritised technology uplift program across three workstreams — integration, occupant experience, and ESG reporting — to be delivered over a 24-month period aligned with the upcoming base building refurbishment.

Background & Context

Leighton Properties engaged Meld Strategies in February 2026 to undertake an independent assessment of the building technology ecosystem at 200 George Street ahead of a planned base building refurbishment program. The assessment scope included review of existing BMS, access control, AV, networking, and tenant amenity systems, alongside stakeholder interviews with the building management team and two anchor tenants.

Findings & Analysis

System Inventory & Integration Status

The building currently operates 11 distinct technology systems across mechanical, electrical, security, and tenant amenity domains. Fewer than 30% of these systems share data via an integration layer, limiting the ability to correlate operational data and create unified occupant experiences.

Key Observations

  • No smart building integration platform (IoT/API middleware) is currently deployed, preventing cross-system data flows and unified analytics.
  • The absence of a tenant experience application places the building behind peer assets in the Sydney CBD for occupant amenity and engagement.
  • Energy metering data is collected but not yet fed into a centralised ESG reporting platform, creating manual reconciliation effort for NABERS compliance.
  • The Gallagher access control system is well-positioned for integration with a future tenant app and visitor management workflow.
  • Car park management system is approaching end of viable life and should be considered for replacement within the refurbishment program.

Recommendations & Next Steps

1
Deploy a Smart Building Integration PlatformProcure and implement an IoT/API middleware layer (e.g. Willow, Mapped, or Johnson Controls OpenBlue) to connect BMS, access control, energy metering, and future tenant systems under a unified data model.
2
Launch a Tenant Experience ApplicationSelect and deploy a tenant app platform (e.g. HqO, Equiem, or Building Stack) integrated with access control and visitor management to improve occupant NPS and support lease renewal outcomes.
3
Implement ESG Data Reporting PipelineConnect energy metering, water, and waste data to a sustainability reporting platform (e.g. Envizi or Measurabl) to automate NABERS data collection and reduce manual reporting effort.
4
Replace End-of-Life Car Park SystemInclude Skidata replacement in the refurbishment program scope, specifying integration capability with the chosen tenant app for seamless parking access and booking.
